# Brainstorm: CoachJack.xyz Full Site Copy Revamp

**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Status:** Ready for planning

---

## What We're Building

A full copy rewrite of coachjack.xyz across all pages (Home, About, Coaching, Contact, Start). The goal is to shift the site's voice from poetic/vulnerable/abstract to professional authority with warmth — while keeping the personal story arc, the somatic/nervous system differentiator, and the problem-first structure that resonates with buyers.

---

## Why This Approach

The current copy reads like a memoir excerpt. Lines like "The mosaic table I'd built — piece by piece, achievement by achievement — was coming apart. I was sleeping in the living room of my own life" are literary but unclear. A prospective C-suite client scanning the site needs to quickly understand: what you do, why you're credible, and what changes for them. The poetic abstraction creates friction where clarity should live.

---

## Key Decisions

1. **Tone: Professional authority** — Lead with credibility and expertise. Personal story is context for authority, not the centerpiece. Still honest and human, but not literary.

2. **Audience: Broaden slightly** — Not just the founder having an existential crisis. Also speak to leaders who want to level up — growth-oriented, not only pain-oriented. Crisis is one entry point, not the only one.

3. **About page: Keep the arc, rewrite it plain** — The wound-to-awakening structure stays, but the language gets concrete and concise. No metaphors that require interpretation. "I burned out in sales leadership, found my way back through yoga and sobriety, now I bring both worlds to coaching" — that level of directness.

4. **Differentiator: Somatic/nervous system stays central** — This IS the differentiator. Frame it with authority and evidence, not mysticism. "I work at the nervous system level" is a clear, powerful claim. "I work where decisions actually get made — your nervous system" works. "The stories you've mistaken for truth" doesn't.

5. **Pain-first structure: Keep it, tone it down** — The Coaching page's problem identification works for the buyer. Just make the language less dramatic and more relatable. Less "the hollow win" poetry, more "you hit the number and wondered why it didn't feel like enough."

6. **Scope: All 5 main pages** — Home, About, Coaching, Contact, Start. Resource/SEO articles can stay as-is (they're already more straightforward informational content).

---

## Page-by-Page Direction

### Home
- **Current issue:** "Status-fied but unfulfilled?" is clever but might alienate broader audience. Philosophy section ("The answer isn't more. It's less.") works but could be sharper.
- **Direction:** Keep the direct address to the reader. Broaden the hook to include growth-seekers alongside crisis-havers. Tighten the differentiator section. Services descriptions are decent — just remove the most abstract language.

### About
- **Current issue:** The biggest offender. Full memoir arc with opaque metaphors. "Mosaic table," "sleeping in the living room of my own life," "radical idea that I didn't need to add anything to become whole."
- **Direction:** Keep the three-act structure (past career, transformation, current work). Make it concrete: what you actually did, what actually happened, what you actually do now. The credentials section is fine. The closing line "I don't coach people to optimize. I coach them to come home." — decide if this stays (it's on the poetic side but has punch).

### Coaching
- **Current issue:** Pain points are vivid but overwrought ("performing a version of yourself that used to fit," "keep solving, keep producing, keep disappearing"). The "How I work" and "What changes" sections are stronger.
- **Direction:** Problem-first structure stays. Simplify the language — make it recognizable, not artful. "You're running a program that used to work" is good and plain. "The performance trap" as a heading is fine. Just rewrite the descriptions underneath.

### Contact
- **Current issue:** Mostly fine. "Tell me what's going on" is good. Minimal changes needed.
- **Direction:** Light touch. Maybe tighten the subhead copy.

### Start (Portal Intake)
- **Current issue:** Also mostly fine. "See How You Actually Operate" works.
- **Direction:** Light touch. Ensure it matches the new tone.

---

## Principles for the Rewrite

1. **If a sentence requires interpretation, rewrite it.** Every line should be clear on first read.
2. **Concrete over abstract.** "I spent 10 years in sales leadership" > "I chased a version of success that looked impressive from the outside."
3. **Authority over vulnerability.** "I know what performance costs because I lived it" > "Inside, something was breaking."
4. **Short sentences.** The current copy uses long, winding constructions. Tighten.
5. **Keep the somatic angle sharp and specific.** "Nervous system regulation" is clear. "The stories you've mistaken for truth" is not.
6. **One metaphor per page, max.** If it's good enough, one lands harder than five.
